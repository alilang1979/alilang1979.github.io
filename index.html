Calculation Overview

This table shows the net premium and max loss for selling put options at various strike prices to achieve a specific annualized yield (e.g., 8%). Strike prices are set at 95%, 90%, 85%, 80%, and 75% of the current market price, rounded down. For each strike:
- Gross Premium per Share = Strike Price × (Expected Yield / 100) × (Days to Expiration / 365).
- Net Premium per Contract = (Gross Premium × 100) - $1.00 (fees), minimum $0.00.
- Max Loss per Contract = (Strike Price × 100) - Net Premium.
The “You will get” amount is the net premium per contract, reflecting proceeds after fees for selling 1 contract.
